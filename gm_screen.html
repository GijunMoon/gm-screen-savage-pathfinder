Situational Rules

Aim: Spend a turn aiming a ranged weapon. On the next turn, ignore up to -4 points of penalties (Range, Cover, Called Shot, Scale) or add +2 to the roll. Cannot take other actions while aiming.

Area Effect Attacks: Place the template and make an arcane skill or Shooting roll. Targets in the template can attempt to Evade. Missed attacks may Deviate.

Breaking Free: From a grapple is an opposed Athletics roll. From a device is a Strength roll, often with a penalty.

Called Shots: Attack a specific location for an effect. Penalties are: Limb (-2), Head/Vitals (-4), Hand/Eye (-6). Success causes a specific extra effect (e.g., target drops item, is Distracted).

Defend: Gain +4 Parry but take no other actions that turn.

Desperate Attack: Gain +2 to an attack roll (Athletics, Fighting, or Shooting) but suffer -2 to the damage roll.

Disarm: A Called Shot (-2) to a foe's weapon. If successful, make an opposed Strength roll. If the attacker wins, the foe drops the weapon.

Deviation: A missed area effect may deviate. Thrown items deviate 1d6", projectiles 2d6". Multiply distance by 2 for Medium, 3 for Long, and 4 for Extreme Range. Roll a d12 for direction (like a clock face). A weapon can't deviate more than half the distance to the original target.

Evasion: Make an Agility roll at -2 to avoid area effect attacks. Success halves damage; a raise avoids it completely.

Finishing Move: A free attack against a helpless foe (+4 to hit). A successful damage roll slays the target, regardless of Wounds.

Firing into Melee: If a ranged attack misses its intended target, a random target adjacent to the original (friend or foe) is hit instead on a 1 on the attack die.

Ganging Up: Foes gain +1 to their Fighting rolls for each additional adjacent attacker, up to a maximum of +4.

Grappling: An opposed Athletics roll to initiate. Success makes the target Vulnerable. A raise makes the target Bound.

Hold: Postpone your action until later in the round. You can interrupt another character's action by winning an opposed Athletics roll.

Illumination: Apply penalties to tasks requiring vision. Dim Light (e.g., twilight) is -2. Dark (starless night) is -4. Pitch Black is -6.

Improvised Weapons: Suffer a -2 penalty to attack and Parry rolls. Damage is typically Str+d4, with a max of Str+d6.

Innocent Bystanders: If a ranged attack misses and the attack die is a 1, a random bystander within 1" of the target is hit instead.

Mounted Combat: Mounted attackers get +1 Fighting vs foot soldiers. Riders must make a Riding roll to stay mounted when their mount is Shaken or Wounded.

Nonlethal Damage: Declare before the attack. A foe who takes a Wound from a nonlethal attack must make a Vigor roll or be knocked unconscious.

Off-Hand Attacks: Attacks made with the off-hand suffer a -2 penalty.

Push: An opposed Strength roll. Success pushes the foe 1", plus 1" for each raise. If pushed into an object, they may become Shaken.

Ranged Weapons in Melee: The intended target adds +2 to their Parry. Firing at another target requires an opposed Agility roll against all adjacent foes.

Reloading: A weapon with a Reload value takes that many actions to reload.

Rest: A full night of rest (approx. 8 hours) removes all levels of Fatigue. Sleeping in medium or heavy armor may prevent recovery and might even cause a level of Fatigue.

Surprise & The Drop: Surprised characters must make a Notice roll or be Shaken. If an attacker has The Drop, they add +4 to their attack and damage rolls, and the target is Vulnerable.

Test: An opposed roll (e.g., Taunt vs. Smarts or Intimidation vs. Spirit) to make a foe Distracted or Vulnerable.

Touch Attack: +2 to a Fighting roll to simply touch a foe (often to deliver a power).

Unstable Platform: -2 penalty to most actions when on an unstable surface.

Wild Attack: Add +2 to a Fighting attack and damage roll, but reduce Parry by -2 until your next action.

Note: The +2 bonus from Desperate Attack and Wild Attack do not stack with each other.

Withdrawing from Melee: Any adjacent foe gets a free Fighting attack against a character who moves out of melee range.

Magic & Powers

Activation: Make an arcane skill roll. Failure costs 1 Power Point and the power fails. Success activates the power and consumes its Power Point cost.

Backlash: A Critical Failure on an activation roll causes a level of Fatigue and all active powers instantly terminate.

Maintaining: Spend 1 Power Point to extend a power by its base Duration.

Recharging: Recover 5 Power Points per hour of rest. May also spend a Benny to regain 5 Power Points as a free action.

Cantrips: An Arcane Background user can perform minor magical effects based on a known power with an arcane skill roll. The effect cannot cause damage.

Magical Healing: The healing power removes one Wound on a success and two with a raise on the arcane skill roll.

Quick Encounters

Used to resolve a scene with a single skill roll per character instead of round-by-round action. The GM describes the scene, players state their actions and choose a skill, then roll. Success is determined narratively.

Suggested Skills by Type:

  • Combat: Fighting, Shooting, arcane skill
  • Crisis: Athletics, Persuasion, Repair
  • Heist/Mission: Stealth, Thievery, Notice, Battle
  • Trek: Survival, Notice, Common Knowledge

Modifiers: The GM assigns a modifier from -4 (very hard) to +4 (very easy) based on the situation, opposition, and equipment.

Conditions

Bleeding Out: Incapacitated characters must make a Vigor roll at the start of their turn. Failure means death. A success keeps them alive. A raise stabilizes them.

Bound: Cannot move or perform any physical action. Parry is 2. Can attempt to break free with a Strength or Agility roll.

Disarmed: The character has dropped a held item. They must take an action to recover it.

Distracted: Suffer -2 to all Trait rolls. Removed at end of turn if no other action is taken.

Entangled: As Bound, but may attempt to break free with a Strength roll at -2.

Fatigued: -1 penalty to all Trait rolls. A second level of Fatigue becomes Exhausted (-2). A third level results in Incapacitation.

Grappled: A grappled character is Vulnerable. If the attacker got a raise, the character is Bound instead.

Incapacitated: Cannot perform any actions and is removed from combat.

Natural Healing: After the “Golden Hour,” characters make a Vigor roll every five days to heal. A success removes one Wound, and a raise removes two.

Poisoned: Suffer effects of a poison. Usually requires a Vigor roll to resist. Effects can include Fatigue, damage, or attribute penalties.

Prone: -2 to Parry. Melee attacks against are at +2; ranged attacks are at -4.

Shaken: Cannot take actions. At the start of their turn, they must make a Spirit roll to recover. A success removes Shaken. A raise removes Shaken and allows them to act.

Stunned: Prone, -2 Parry, cannot act or move. At start of turn, make a Spirit roll to recover (at -2 if from physical damage).

Vulnerable: All actions against the target are at +2. Lasts until the end of their next turn.

Wounded: Each Wound inflicts a -1 penalty to Pace and all Trait rolls (max -3). Three Wounds means Incapacitated. Within the “Golden Hour” (1 hr of injury), a Healing roll can remove one Wound (two on a raise).

Dramatic Tasks

Used to resolve complex situations under a time limit. The GM sets the skill, number of rounds, and Task Points needed (usually 5). Each round, characters make a skill roll.

  • Success: Gain 1 Task Point.
  • Raise: Gain 2 Task Points.
  • Failure: No points. A Complication occurs (e.g., -1 penalty to the next round's roll).
  • Critical Failure: Lose one previously earned Task Point (if any). A Complication also occurs.

Support: Allies can make Support rolls to aid the primary character. A success adds +1 to the lead's roll, and a raise adds +2 (max total bonus of +4).

The party succeeds if they gather enough Task Points in time, and fails if the time runs out.
